TOPIC: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

Title: UPI Payment Fraud Targeting College Students

UPI payment fraud is a growing cybercrime in India, where scammers trick users into transferring money to fake accounts. Typically, the fraud begins with a message or call claiming to be from a bank or popular app like Google Pay or PhonePe. The scammer asks the student to share OTPs or approve a transaction, often under a false sense of urgency.

College students are commonly targeted due to their frequent use of digital payments for food, transport, and online subscriptions. In one fictional scenario, a student received a WhatsApp message stating their account was temporarily locked. Believing it was genuine, the student shared the OTP, allowing the scammer to transfer ₹5,000 instantly. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering money, along with potential identity exposure.

Awareness and caution are key to prevention. Students should never share OTPs, verify official communication, and double-check transaction details before approving payments. Understanding how such scams work can help students protect themselves and report incidents quickly.
